Overview

Purpose

I want to send a message people with a passion for fashion, but may not have the means to support the growth of a conventional wardrobe. I want to contribute to a change in the view that fashion has to be new or luxury. Second hand fashion is a great way to express yourself, give life to an old piece of clothing, and subsequently reduce the environmental impact of discarded clothing. Did you know that the amount of textile waste created annually is an estimated 92 million tons? By inspiring fashion-oriented individuals to pursue this kind of reuse, the emotional and environmental effects abound. I would love to create a blog where there is a community that is passionate about second hand fashion, and a community that cares about the world we live in, even if it is a residual idea from becoming more involved in the second hand community.

Audience

I want to market towards college age, teens to young adults. I want to reach people who are just beginning to understand and know who they really are, and are can share in the feeling of becoming an adult. I want to connect with a population of people who care.

Commitment to nature:
Every year, nearly 13 million tons of textile waste is discarded into landfills, and this is only partially due to commercial retailers. The average citizen of the Earth plays a large role in the generation of textile waste, and there is something we can do about it, and if you're here, you'll like the solution! No doubt that ever since you were small, you knew what the thrift store was, or you dreaded that the only new clothes you would get that year would be from your older siblings. Well the era of being embarrassed by secondhand clothing is coming to a close. Not only is shopping for or wearing secondhand clothing minimizing the impact we have on the Earth, but it is also a cheap ticket to feeling confident in your second skin: what you wear. The way we dress is much more important than we may realize (you can read more about this phenomenon here). By even just encouraging others to think more about secondhand options, you can be the beginning of a new era for our Earth and for yourself. Feeling confident in your own skin (your first, and second ;)) is a priority that we often push aside because of the events and situations outside of us telling us that our self-esteem can wait, but it is important to realize that a better world for all of us begins with the only person that we can truly make choices for--ourselves.

Blog -- Who am I?

Hello beautiful people, I'm Jojo! I'm a connoisseur of everything second hand fashion has always been an important part of my life, even if I wasn't necessarily up to date with the trends, the way I dressed was always a way that I could show people who I was without saying it outright. I was a relatively silent child, and only really began speaking when I was in middle school, but the way I dressed always gave me the voice I often felt like I didn't have.

Though fashion is a passion of mine, it isn't the only thing that makes me who I am! I love so many things and flit from hobby to hobby—as I've noticed has become quite normal for my generation—but there are a couple things that have really stuck with me! I love crocheting, knitting and sewing, rock climbing, writing stories and poems (as a journaling device :D), and scrapbooking. I feel like a lot of these hobbies translate well to my passion, so I think it's a good fit! But you might be saying, where does rock climbing fit in? Well, it doesn't necessarily “fit in,” but I do like to get creative with my climbing outfits sometimes as well! That's what I love so much about styling yourself—anywhere you go, you can show people who you are without having to say it!

I have three siblings and a mom and dad who I adore, and of course, there's also my baby, Ollie! We adopted Oliver almost 7 years ago, and he'll be eight in December. He is the love and light of my life, and being away from him for college is very difficult, but I have to keep in mind that he would love the growth that I am experiencing while I'm here, even if he doesn't get to see me half the year. Ollie is a big part of my life, and so is my family, so of course they would need to be in my about me post! I'm the youngest of 4 awesome kiddos (who aren't so much kiddos anymore ;D), and I would say one of my best friends is my brother, the second youngest. Out of the four, we've had the most time to grow up together, and he's taught me so much in my short 18 years. My mom and my dad are amazing too, they are the kind of people to instantly adopt anyone who walks through their door, loving them quickly and without judgment (me and my brother call them saints, but they refuse the title—too humble! xD). My older two siblings are like a second set of parents to me. Even though I don't see them very often anymore, they've both taught me so much, and I wouldn't trade them for anyone else in the world.

I could talk all I want about myself, on and on about my awesome friends, my life, my dog, how much I've grown as a person, etc etc, but what I really find important about starting this website is encouraging others to look at the good in their lives. I've always loved journaling, and the first thing I always suggest to people wanting to journal is to begin to write stories about your own life—how you remember things, how your life has changed, what you do that makes you, you, and how that effects your self esteem. I am always in a place of trying to become a better me, and even if it slows me down for just a second, I know that I am growing and stretching my physical, emotional, and mental muscles, and I can attest that even through all the hard times, I feel like I get stronger everyday, physically, emotionally, and mentally; even though sometimes my 'muscles' can feel 'sore.'
